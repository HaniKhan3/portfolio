Quaid-e-Azam

Quaid-e-Azam, also known as Muhammad Ali Jinnah, was the founding father of Pakistan. He played a pivotal role in the independence movement and negotiations with the British for the creation of Pakistan in 1947. Jinnah served as Pakistan's first Governor-General until his death in 1948. He is widely revered as a visionary leader and is often referred to as the "Father of the Nation" in Pakistan
